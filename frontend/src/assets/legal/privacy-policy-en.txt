2. Subject matter and purposes of processing personal data

We process personal data solely in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the Digital Services Act (DSA), and applicable national data protection laws.

a) Account and profile data (if provided voluntarily)

To provide the service’s features, display user-generated content, and technically administer the user account.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR


---

b) User-generated content (as amended)

Posts, comments, attachments, embedded content, and related metadata are processed in order to provide the platform, display content, moderate content, and protect against misuse.

For moderation purposes, automated procedures including AI-assisted detection may be used.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

c) Device and usage data

This includes in particular IP address, timestamps, technical headers, and—if enabled by you—coarse location information.

Processing is carried out for:

technical delivery of the service

ensuring system security

abuse and fraud prevention

error analysis and stability assurance


Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

d) Local storage on your device

To provide offline features, performance optimization, and reliability, we store data locally in the browser or the app (e.g., IndexedDB, local storage, cache).

This data generally remains on your device unless active synchronization or export takes place.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

e) Contact and support requests

If you contact us, we process the data you provide to handle your request.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) or Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

f) Consent and preference settings

To document and implement your choices (e.g., privacy, content, or usage settings).

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(c) and Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR


---

3. Admin backend and internal evaluations

a) Admin backend

The admin backend is not publicly accessible and is restricted to authorized persons only.
Use by end users is excluded.


---

b) Anonymized usage statistics

Within the admin area, anonymized statistical evaluations are stored regarding how often individual backend routes are accessed.

This data:

is fully anonymized

does not contain IP addresses or user identifiers

does not allow conclusions to be drawn about individual persons


Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

c) Server and system logs

Server log files are stored for a maximum of 7 days and then automatically deleted.

Log entries transmitted to the admin backend are stored for a maximum of 2 days.

Storage is carried out exclusively for security, error analysis, and abuse prevention purposes.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR

ADDENDUM TO THE PRIVACY POLICY

Digital Services Act (DSA)

Last updated: 14 December 2025


---

1. Scope

This addendum applies to all processing of personal data in connection with notices of unlawful content, moderation measures, decisions, and complaints pursuant to the Digital Services Act (DSA).


---

2. Notices of unlawful content (Notices)

When submitting a notice, we process in particular:

information about the reported content

time of the notice

voluntarily provided reasoning

voluntarily provided contact details (name, email address)


Providing name and email address is voluntary and serves communication within the procedure.

Legal basis:
Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR
Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

3. Public case page

For each DSA-relevant procedure, a publicly accessible case page may be provided.

It is intended for:

the reporting user

the affected user (accused)

competent authorities


In particular, the following may be displayed on the case page:

information about the reported content

procedural status

decisions taken

reasons pursuant to the DSA


Publication is carried out exclusively within the scope of statutory transparency obligations.


---

4. Moderation decisions and permanent storage

Moderation decisions, reasons, procedural steps, and related metadata are stored permanently insofar as this is required to fulfill statutory documentation, evidence, and accountability obligations under the Digital Services Act.

No automatic deletion of this data takes place.

Legal basis:
Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR
Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR


---

5. Complaints and legal remedies (Appeals)

If a complaint is filed, we process the data provided for the implementation and documentation of the procedure.

The data is stored beyond completion of the procedure insofar as this is legally required.


---

6. Export and disclosure to authorities

Competent authorities may be provided with an export option for relevant procedure data to fulfill statutory information and cooperation obligations.

Disclosure is carried out exclusively within the legally permissible framework.


---

7. Access restrictions

DSA-relevant data is accessible only to authorized persons within the internal admin and moderation area.
