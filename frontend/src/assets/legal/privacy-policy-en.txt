###
Summary (not legally binding)
###

MessageDrop is a privacy-focused web app for publishing user-generated content, private communication, and location-based organization of information.

The service can be used without a user account. Certain features require the voluntary creation of a user account.
Login is performed locally via a PIN; the PIN is not transmitted to the server. Only the pseudonymous user ID, public keys, and status data are stored on the server in the user account.

Use of MessageDrop is permitted only after reaching the age of 16. Persons who have reached the age of 16 but have not yet reached the age of 18 may use the service only with the consent of their parents or legal guardians. Legally binding declarations that go beyond the ordinary use of the service are generally only possible upon reaching the age of 18.

A large portion of the data is intentionally stored primarily locally on the user’s device (e.g., in the browser or app storage) to ensure data minimization and a high standard of data protection. Control over this locally stored data generally lies with the user. Loss or deletion of local data is possible at any time.

The optional read-aloud feature uses the browser’s or operating system’s speech synthesis. For this purpose, the text selected by the user is passed to the speech component of the device or platform. Depending on the browser, operating system, device, and selected voice, processing may take place locally on the device or via components of the platform provider.

Without a previously created backup, it is not possible to restore locally deleted data.

Public content and comments are created by users and may be reported, reviewed, and moderated in accordance with the provisions of the Digital Services Act (DSA). Content for which DSA proceedings are initiated will be temporarily hidden from public view.

Personal information must not be published publicly—neither about oneself nor about other individuals.

Potentially illegal public content can be reported via the reporting function within the service or through the DSA contact point listed in the legal notice. Affected users may—to the extent provided by law—file internal complaints against moderation decisions.

MessageDrop does not use a personalized recommendation or ranking system. Public content is not algorithmically filtered or prioritized, but is displayed exclusively based on the geographical area being viewed and the time of publication.

Private chat messages are transmitted with end-to-end encryption and are generally not visible to us in plain text.

External embeds (e.g., YouTube, TikTok, Pinterest, Spotify, Tenor, Unsplash) are loaded only after the user has actively enabled the respective provider. Blanket consent for all providers is not available.

This is separate from external functional services (e.g., Open-Meteo, OpenStreetMap/Nominatim, DeepL, Viator), which are used when the respective function is actively used and are not enabled via the external content toggle.

When Unsplash images are selected, image files can be loaded, downloaded to the device, and stored locally.

Consent granted for external content may be revoked at any time with future effect (e.g., by deactivating the respective provider in the settings).

System messages (e.g., notices regarding DSA proceedings or scheduled maintenance) are generated exclusively by authorized administrative systems and displayed in the user’s frontend. System messages are always active; users can delete them in the frontend without this necessarily affecting internal records.

Profiles (name/avatar) can be used locally for easier identification. When initiating contact, a user may optionally request profile sharing; the other user must actively consent. Once consent has been given, the name and avatar are stored locally on the contact’s device.

Places can be followed; where technically possible and supported by the device, the user may receive push notifications when new public content appears at a followed place. Technically required push tokens are processed solely for delivery. There is no entitlement to the delivery of push notifications.

MessageDrop offers optional Usage Time Protection (self-restriction or parental mode) with a daily limit and optional time windows. A parental PIN is processed exclusively as a cryptographic hash; the plaintext PIN is not stored.

MessageDrop does not provide access to emergency services.

Only the current, complete German version of this Privacy Policy, including the additions relating to the Digital Services Act (DSA), is authoritative.

###
Glossary
###

User
A natural person who uses MessageDrop, regardless of whether a user account has been created.

User Account
A technical, pseudonymous access unit that a user may voluntarily create and that is identified by a User ID.

User ID
Technical identifier of a user account. The User ID is pseudonymous and, on its own, does not allow for the direct identification of a person.

Visitor
A user who uses MessageDrop without creating a user account.

Public Content
Content created by users (e.g., public messages and comments) that is generally publicly visible unless it has been restricted or removed.

Private Communication
Communication between users that is transmitted in encrypted form (e.g., private chats).

External Content / Third-Party Providers
Content provided by third-party providers and integrated only after an active decision by the user (e.g., iframes/oEmbed). Upon activation, data is transmitted to the respective provider.

System Messages
Internal messages generated exclusively by authorized administrative systems and displayed in the frontend (e.g., regarding DSA proceedings or maintenance work). System messages do not constitute an email inbox.

DSA Proceedings
Proceedings under the Digital Services Act (DSA), in particular notices and appeals in connection with public content.

Public Case Page
A publicly accessible page relating to DSA proceedings on which only anonymized or non-personal information is published.

Push Notifications / Push Tokens
Technical feature for delivering notifications to a device. Push tokens are technical identifiers that may be required for delivery.

Local Storage
Storage of data on the user’s device (e.g., IndexedDB, app storage, cache). This data can be deleted by the user or due to technical circumstances.

Read-Aloud Function
Optional feature that allows selected text to be read aloud using the browser’s or operating system’s text-to-speech synthesis. Which voices are available and whether processing occurs locally or on the platform depends on the browser, operating system, device, and selected voice.

Usage Time Protection
Optional feature for limiting usage time (in “self” or “parental” mode) with a daily limit and optional time windows.

Parent PIN Hash
Cryptographic hash value of a parent PIN set by the user in the parental mode of Usage Time Protection. The plaintext PIN is not stored.

Whether speech synthesis takes place entirely locally on the device or whether components of the browser, operating system, or platform provider are involved depends on the specific technical environment, in particular on:

- operating system,
- device,
- installed language packs,
- selected voice.

We have no influence over the technical design or any data processing by browser, operating system, or platform providers in connection with their speech synthesis. ###
3. Use with a User Account
###

###
3.1 Creating a User Account
###

Creating a user account is voluntary and occurs solely by assigning a PIN.

The server initially creates only a pseudonymous user ID.

Cryptographic key pairs for encryption and signing are generated on the device. The public keys are stored in the user account on the server; private keys remain exclusively on the device.

The following are stored on the server:
- the pseudonymous user ID,
- public signature and encryption keys,
- a user status (e.g., enabled or disabled),
- the date of the last login, as well as technically necessary status or metadata.

All other user data is stored exclusively in encrypted form locally on the device.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract or implementation of pre-contractual measures at the user’s request).

###
3.2 Login and Authentication
###

For login, the PIN is used exclusively locally to decrypt the encrypted user data record on the device.

The device then requests a one-time verification challenge from the server. This challenge is signed with the local private signature key. The server verifies the signature and, if the verification is successful, provides a technical access token for subsequent communication.

The PIN and the decrypted user data record are not transmitted to the server.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of the login and authentication process).

###
3.2.1 Security Reset (Key Regeneration)
###

Users can generate new key pairs for security reasons (security reset). In this process, the public keys in the user account are updated; private keys remain stored locally.

During the security reset, the chat history is deleted, but contacts are retained. Contacts who are online receive the new public keys immediately; other contacts receive them upon the next update.

###
3.3 Deletion and Inactivity
###

User accounts that have not been logged into for a period of 90 days are removed from the backend without warning.

Upon removal of the user account, all associated server-side data records are deleted, in particular:
- the user ID,
- the user status,
- login and activity information,
- other uniquely identifiable server-side references.

The deletion is final unless a previously created backup of the user’s data exists.

Regardless of this, the user may delete their user account independently via the app at any time. In this case, all server-side and local data records will be deleted immediately and permanently, subject to statutory retention or evidentiary obligations (e.g., in connection with DSA proceedings).

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision, administration, and deletion of the user account) as well as Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (compliance with legal obligations), to the extent that legal obligations require further processing.

###
3.4 Public Content and Comments
###

Users may post public messages and public comments.

Public content must not contain any personal information, regardless of whether it pertains to the user or others. Such content may be moderated, hidden, or removed.

This content is processed for the following purposes:
- Provision and display within the service,
- Moderation and enforcement of usage rules,
- Prevention of abuse and fraud,
- Compliance with legal obligations, in particular under the DSA.

To support moderation, content (including hashtags) may be analyzed automatically. If technically necessary, an external AI moderation service may be involved. This may involve the transmission of content data and technical metadata to the respective service provider. We use the results exclusively to support moderation and to fulfill legal obligations.

Public messages and public comments are generally deleted after 30 days. There is no entitlement to permanent availability, archiving, or restoration.

Public content may be rated (e.g., like/dislike). There is no entitlement to any particular reach, visibility, or rating outcome.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision and processing of public content and comments) as well as Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in operation, security, and prevention of misuse) as well as Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (compliance with legal obligations), to the extent that legal obligations apply.

###
3.5 Private Communication
###

Private chat messages are transmitted using end-to-end encryption.

Read private messages are generally deleted automatically after 7 days. There is no entitlement to availability, archiving, or restoration.

The users involved are solely responsible for the content of private communications. There is no prior review of content or ongoing monitoring. However, procedural measures (e.g., blocking/removal) may result from legal obligations, to the extent necessary in individual cases.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision and operation of private communication) as well as—to the extent that procedural measures are necessary to fulfill legal obligations—Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (compliance with legal obligations).

###
3.6 Local Data Storage on the Device
###

MessageDrop follows a privacy-oriented approach in which the majority of data is primarily stored locally on the user’s device.

This includes, in particular:
- local user data,
- settings and preferences,
- contacts and links,
- locally cached content,
- cryptographic keys,
- temporary working data.

Depending on the platform, storage occurs primarily via the browser’s IndexedDB, local app storage, and cache mechanisms of the operating system or browser.

Locally stored data is not automatically synchronized with other devices or a cloud.

Control over locally stored data lies with the user. The user can delete this data at any time, in particular by using the corresponding functions within the app, deleting app or browser data, uninstalling the app or the installed web app, or resetting the browser or device.

Without the user’s involvement, we have no access to locally stored data and no way to view, back up, or subsequently restore this locally stored data.

If locally stored data is deleted by the user or due to technical circumstances, this may result in the loss of content, settings, or access. We cannot restore this data without a backup previously created by the user.

The responsibility for backing up, preserving, and managing locally stored data lies exclusively with the user.

###
3.6.1 Backup and Restore Function (optional)
###

To reduce the risk of data loss, MessageDrop provides an optional backup and restore function.

As part of the backup function, the user selects a local storage location and a personal PIN. The backup is stored in encrypted form. The PIN used is known exclusively to the user and is neither stored by us nor recoverable by us. If the PIN is lost or forgotten, it is technically impossible to restore the backup.

The data contained in the backup corresponds to the data assigned to the user and stored locally and on the server at the time the backup is created.

When restoring a backup, any existing user account is permanently deleted both locally and on the server. All associated data is removed and then restored based on the backup. There is no merging of existing and restored data.

As part of the restore process, the data contained in the backup is transferred back to the server and—where technically implemented—created or restored there. Discrepancies between the original state and the state after the restore are possible.

Backups are tied to the key state at the time of creation. After a security reset, older backups may be considered obsolete and may not be restorable.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision and use of the backup/restore function at the user’s request).

###
3.7 Local Profile, Profile Sharing, and Contact Profiles
###

The user can create a local profile (e.g., name and avatar) for their own user account. This profile is generally stored exclusively locally on the device.

Within the context of the contact function, a user can actively request profile sharing with a contact. The contact must actively confirm the profile sharing (e.g., via a pop-up) before a transfer takes place.

Once consent is granted, the profile name and avatar are transferred to the contact’s device and stored locally there. We do not store this profile information centrally.

Note: After the transfer, there is no technical way to retrieve or delete the profile information already transferred from the contact’s device. The locally stored data can only be deleted by the receiving contact on their device.

In addition, each user can independently set their own local profile entries for contacts (e.g., their own display name, their own avatar). This information is only visible locally.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision of the contact function) and—to the extent that profile sharing is based on voluntary, active consent—Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent of the data subject to the processing of personal data concerning them).

###
3.8 System Messages
###

MessageDrop provides users with system messages. System messages are internal communications generated exclusively by authorized administrative systems (in particular via the admin and moderation system) and transmitted to the user’s frontend via the MessageDrop backend.

System messages serve in particular to:
- provide information regarding proceedings under the Digital Services Act (DSA),
- communicate decisions, procedural statuses, or measures,
- provide information regarding technical or organizational processes (e.g., scheduled maintenance, security-related notices, or system-related restrictions).

System messages are always active and cannot be disabled.

In the context of system messages, we process in particular:
- User ID,
- content of the message,
- times of creation, provision, and delivery of the message,
- delivery and read status (to the extent technically implemented).

Users can delete system messages in the frontend. Deletion affects only the display or local availability in the user’s frontend. Legally required or documentation-mandated records (e.g., in the context of DSA proceedings) may remain unaffected by this.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision of system messages in connection with the use of the service) and Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (compliance with legal obligations), to the extent that system messages are required for this purpose (e.g., DSA).

###
3.9 Following Places and Push Notifications
###

Users can follow places or stop following them.

Where technically possible and supported by the device, users can receive push notifications when new public content is posted at a place they follow.

For push notifications, technically necessary tokens/identifiers are processed depending on the platform so that messages can be delivered to the device (e.g., push tokens). These tokens are processed for delivery purposes and are not used by us for profiling or tracking purposes.

Push notifications may be delayed, restricted, or not delivered for technical, system-related, or user-specific reasons; there is no entitlement to delivery.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (the “Follow” function) as well as Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent of the data subject to the processing of personal data concerning them), provided that the use of push notifications is designed to be consent-based or requires system-level authorization on the device.

###
3.10 Usage Time Protection (Self-Restriction / Parental Mode)
###

MessageDrop offers optional Usage Time Protection with the following basic functions:
- “Off” mode (deactivated),
- “Self” mode (self-restriction with daily limit and configurable self-extension),
- “Parental” mode (daily limit and configurable extension via parent PIN).

The following data is processed for this purpose in particular:
- Usage Time Protection settings (mode, daily limit, time slots per weekday, extension duration, and allowed number of extensions),
- Local daily status (date, usage seconds consumed, and number of self- and parent-initiated extensions already used),
- Optionally, a parent PIN hash (no plaintext PIN).

The parent PIN is exclusively hashed locally (cryptographic hash) and is not stored in plain text. For logged-in user accounts, settings and status data for Usage Time Protection can be transferred to the backend for synchronization.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (provision of the requested protection feature) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in youth protection-oriented and responsible use).

###
4. Digital Services Act (DSA)
###

###
4.1 DSA Proceedings
###

To the extent that the legal requirements of the Digital Services Act (DSA) apply to MessageDrop, proceedings may be initiated in relation to public content and comments, in particular through notices or appeals.

Notices may be submitted via the reporting function within the service or through the DSA contact point listed in the legal notice.

To facilitate prompt processing, a notice should be as specific as possible (e.g., link/ID of the content, brief explanation, and optional supporting evidence).

In this context, we process in particular:
- User ID (if available),
- information about the person submitting the notice, to the extent voluntarily provided (e.g., email address),
- information about the reported content,
- timestamps relating to the proceedings (e.g., receipt of the notice, processing, decision, notification, and conclusion of the proceedings),
- status of the proceedings,
- decisions taken and reasons given,
- information from internal complaints against moderation decisions (to the extent provided for by law).

Additionally, the reporting person may voluntarily provide evidence as part of a DSA procedure, in particular:
- file uploads (e.g., screenshots or documents),
- external links as evidence.

File uploads are stored on our servers. External links are stored in our database. This evidence is processed exclusively for the purpose of reviewing the reported facts and for documentation in accordance with legal obligations and is not made publicly available.

As part of moderation decisions, we also process data to notify affected users (e.g., regarding the status of the proceedings, measures taken, key reasons for the decision, and, where applicable, the involvement of automated processes, to the extent legally permissible and technically feasible).

Data regarding obviously abusive reports or complaints (e.g., serial reports without factual basis) may be processed for the purpose of preventing abuse.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (legal obligation) and Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in enforcing rights, preventing abuse, and retaining evidence).

###
4.2 Public Case Page
###

A publicly accessible case page may be provided for DSA-related proceedings.

This page contains only anonymized or non-personal information, specifically the status of the proceedings, the nature of the reported content, decisions made, and legal justifications in accordance with the DSA.

No personal data is published. Publication is solely for the purpose of fulfilling legal transparency obligations.

###
4.3 Public DSA Statistics
###

MessageDrop can provide publicly accessible, aggregated statistics showing the number of proceedings initiated, the nature of the decisions, and the ratio of notices to measures taken.

The statistics do not contain any personal data and do not allow for any conclusions to be drawn about individual users.

###
4.4 Storage and Disclosure
###

DSA-related moderation, decision, and procedural data are stored to the extent necessary to fulfill legal documentation, evidence, and accountability obligations.

Unless administrative or judicial proceedings are pending, DSA-related procedural data (e.g., notices, decisions, and associated metadata) will be deleted no later than six months after the conclusion of the respective proceeding.

Evidence (files and external links) voluntarily provided in the context of a DSA proceeding is stored only for as long as it is necessary for the conduct of the proceeding and for statutory verification and documentation obligations, and is also deleted no later than six months after the conclusion of the proceeding, unless further retention is required.

If administrative or judicial proceedings are initiated in connection with a DSA proceeding, or if such proceedings are foreseeable, the relevant data may be stored for the duration of those proceedings and beyond within the legally permissible scope (so-called Legal Hold). In this case, deletion shall occur immediately once the reason for retention no longer applies.

Disclosure to competent authorities, courts, or other authorized bodies shall take place exclusively within the legally required and permissible scope.

The legal basis is Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR (fulfillment of legal documentation, evidence, transparency, and disclosure obligations) as well as Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in maintaining evidence and in the assertion, exercise, or defense of legal claims), to the extent that storage or disclosure is necessary beyond the immediate legal obligation.

To the extent that, in individual cases within the scope of a DSA proceeding, special categories of personal data within the meaning of Art. 9 GDPR or personal data relating to criminal convictions and offenses within the meaning of Art. 10 GDPR are included, their processing shall take place exclusively to the extent that this is legally permissible and necessary for the conduct, documentation, or disclosure of the proceeding.

###
5. Retention Period
###

Personal data is stored only for as long as necessary for the respective purpose or as required by law.

This does not apply to DSA procedural data (including necessary evidence/supporting documents), which may be subject to statutory retention obligations or documentation-related requirements.

In particular, the following principles apply:
- Visitor/server logs: storage as necessary for security and operation (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR (legitimate interest in security and operation)),
- User account metadata (user ID, status, last login): until the user account is deleted, subject to legal obligations,
- Usage Time Protection (settings/status, including parent PIN hash): until modified/deleted by the user or until the user account is deleted,
- public content: deletion after 30 days,
- read private messages: deletion after 7 days,
- locally stored data: deletion by the user or due to technical circumstances is possible.

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from loss, misuse, or unauthorized access. However, complete protection cannot be guaranteed.
