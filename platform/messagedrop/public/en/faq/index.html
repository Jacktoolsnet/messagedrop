General

These first questions cover the basic ideas behind MessageDrop.

What is MessageDrop?

MessageDrop is a location-based web app. You can leave public messages at real-world places on a map, follow places that matter to you, and use private content and communication features.

What can I use MessageDrop for?

You can use MessageDrop to share information connected to places, discover current messages nearby, keep private notes, follow important places, and connect with people you already know.

Do I have to register to use MessageDrop?

You can open MessageDrop without a classic email-and-password registration. Some functions need a local user identity or a connection to the backend so that your own content can be assigned and synchronized securely.

Is MessageDrop free?

MessageDrop can currently be used without a usage fee. Costs charged by your internet provider, mobile provider, device, app store, or third-party services are not controlled by MessageDrop.

How old do I have to be to use MessageDrop?

You must be at least 16 years old to use MessageDrop. If additional legal requirements apply in your country, you must also follow those requirements.

Is MessageDrop available in multiple languages?

Yes. MessageDrop supports multiple languages. German and English are the main languages for the public pages and legal information; the app also contains additional language options.

Who is MessageDrop for?

MessageDrop is for people who want to connect information with places: private users, friends, local communities, travelers, creators, and anyone who wants a map-based way to share or remember context.

Do I need to install an app to use MessageDrop?

No separate app installation is required. MessageDrop runs in the browser and can be opened as a web app. Depending on your device and browser, you may also be able to add it to your home screen.

Does MessageDrop work on smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers?

Yes. MessageDrop is designed as a responsive web app and can be used on smartphones, tablets, and desktop browsers. Some functions, such as location access, depend on your device and browser permissions.

Who operates MessageDrop?

MessageDrop is operated by JackTools.Net UG (limited liability). You can find the current provider details and contact information in the legal notice.

Is MessageDrop a messenger?

MessageDrop is not a classic messenger or social feed. The core idea is to connect messages and information with places. Private communication exists, but the app is built around places rather than around follower counts or algorithmic feeds.
